Burrito

Description

Burrito is a dish in Mexican and Tex-Mex cuisine that took form in Mexican cuisine, consisting of a flour tortilla wrapped into a sealed cylindrical shape around various ingredients.

The tortilla is sometimes lightly grilled or steamed to soften it, make it more pliable, and allow it to adhere to itself. Burritos are often eaten by hand, as their tight wrapping keeps the ingredients together. Burritos can also be served "wet", i.e., covered in a savory and spicy sauce, when they would be eaten with a fork and knife.

Ingredients

  • Flour tortillas
  • Meat
  • Beans or refried beans
  • Cheese
  • Rice
  • Lettuce
  • Guacamole
  • Salsa
  • Sour cream

Steps

  1. Warm the flour tortilla to help it stretch.
  2. Arrange the fillings in a line down the center.
  3. Fold the sides inwards, over the filling.
  4. Roll the burrito over the filling, tucking as you roll.
  5. Continue rolling until your burrito is seam side down and serve it.
